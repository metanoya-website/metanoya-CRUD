By Palak Grover / April 10th 2022

Unlike many physical and visible disabilities such as Cerebral Palsy and Intellectual Impairment existing around us, it makes Dyslexia one of the most underrated and ignored disability, despite being highlighted in movies such as, “ Taare Zameen Par” yet failing to strike a chord with the society almost a decade later.

Inorder to understand this umbrella term better, let us first understand its literal meaning.

Dyslexia is a language communication disability. It originates from the Greek words “Dys” meaning defective or difficult and 'Lexia' meaning language. If we combine the lateral words together it means, “difficulty with words”.
Dyslexia is neurological and tends to run in families. It gives rise to problems in reading, writing, spelling and comprehension. Since it is not a disease, it has no cure.

Among Learning Disabilities the one that has become most widely known is Dyslexia. This disability affects one of our life's major activities which is education and where there is a glitch in education, life stands still, especially for a parent!

About 80% of the students with learning disabilities are dyslexic. However this doesn't mean that these children are low on IQ or intellect, rather it has been observed that most of the children who have been diagnosed are very well versed in other domains such as music, dance, sports and even theatre. It is therefore important to understand that the Special Educators, Psychologists, parents and teachers undertake the task of decoding any given diagnosis related to learning disability as clearly and as early as possible.

This can be done by following specific and structured means of intervention from screening till implementation
- Screening
- Identification
- Assessment
- Diagnosis
- Interpretation
- Interest
- Inclusivity

One may think that it may be a one man job, but it takes an entire team of professionals and stakeholders to decode a simple diagnosis. The above mentioned pointers not only set the right path of intervention, but also focus on reducing the academic lag caused by certain learning disabilities.
Dyslexia and other related learning disabilities suffer for long as there aren't any specific physical hindrances or handicaps seen or observed in a child. However, when the stakeholders are well versed with the specific red flags or characteristic features regarding the same, the ride to intervention becomes smoother for the Special Educator and especially the child.

As mentioned above, none of the Learning Disabilities can be cured, so it's very important for the guardian to understand and follow the intervention program with discipline and keep practicing patience, stability, trust, motivation and acceptance of the child. Highlighting on what 'my child can do' rather than, 'what he or she is not able to do.’ plays a key role not only in acceptance of what the diagnosis is, but also highlighting in an academic obsessed world that other functional domains can also lead up to a happy and independent life when channelised properly.

Invisible or not, it is upto our generation and professionals to make Dyslexia more than just an umbrella term for reading- writing disabilities and make it visible for what all a child or an individual can achieve despite having it.

Developmental Dyslexia is a learning disorder characterised by difficulty reading. Dyslexia occurs in children with normal vision and intelligence. Symptoms include late talking, learning new words slowly and a delay in learning to read.
