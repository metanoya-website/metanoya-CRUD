Co-Occurence & Neurodiversity

CO-EXISTING SPECTRUM - OVERLAPS AND MASKING -

There have been several reports of overlapping between dyslexia and a range of neurodevelopmental disorders of other kind. specifically symptoms of attention-deficit with hyperactivity disorder, atypical sensory perception, autistic spectrum disorder and developmental coordination disorders are found to co-exist.

Incidences of dyscalculia, dysgraphia, dyspraxia, anxiety, depression and tantrums are too closely associated with developmental dyslexia.
Normal to High IQ in dyslexics frequently masks the disability for long making diagnosis delayed and ambiguous.

The heritability of dyslexia is estimated around 52%, which is very high and the male to female ratio is 1.5:1.
Researches have revealed that there is more than an eight-fold increase in NDPs with in dyslexics when compared to the typical / normal readers.

It is found in various studies that the symptoms of inattention, verbal language problems and atypical sensory perceptions are significantly associated with dyslexia.
As dyslexia often overlaps with other NDPs. Thus the study of behavioural profiles of co-occurring problems while understanding the pattern of strengths and needs in a kid with dyslexia becomes very important prior to intervention to achieve maximum results.

Emotional issues might be seen in a number of cases, such as disruptive behaviour, throwing tantrums or in physical symptoms such as stomach aches or palpitations, and refusing to go to school. Thus the parental education for the awareness of same and training to deal with them becomes essential. Seeking help at the right time is the key.
Considering all these issues METANOYA provides a holistic care module for dyslexics.

NEURODIVERSITY -

The revolutionary concept simply means the differences of neurological abilities between individuals which defines their learning behaviour, creativity, thinking skills, emotional aspects and also perceptions to some extent.

This new term encompasses the SpLDs which often overlap with dyslexia and gives more respectful social refection to them instead of negative connotations of neurotypical society which narrows down resources and opportunities for inclusive environment.

Effort is to makes society welcoming for all neurotypes and help promote growth and create platform of contribution from all to achieve larger developmental goals.
